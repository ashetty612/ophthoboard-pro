Total Time & Cases

--- Slide 11: Exam Structure ---

Exam Structure

- 42 cases per exam
  - 14 cases x 3 appointments
  - 7 cases per subject area
- Each appointment is 50 minutes long
- Examiners manage the clock, not candidates

--- Slide 12: Section Header ---

About Examiners

--- Slide 13: Examiner Qualifications ---

Examiner Qualifications

- Board certified in good standing
- Willing to devote significant time and expertise
- Completes extensive training on examining
- Adheres to strict guidelines on scoring
- Commits to principles of fairness and professional behavior
- Attends unconscious bias training

--- Slide 14: Examiner Responsibilities ---

Examiner Responsibilities

- Evaluation of candidate performance

--- Slide 21: More About Performance ---

More About Performance

- Each case contributes equally to your score. There are no trick questions or
  automatic failures!
- Scoring is "compensatory," meaning strong performance in one area can compensate
  for poor performance in another.
- Passing or failing the exam is based on whether your performance meets the
  standard -- not how anyone else performs.
- Results are pass/fail only.

--- Slide 29: Section Header ---

Troubleshooting

--- Slide 30: "What If" Situations ---

"What If" Situations

I need an unscheduled break.
  You can take additional break time, but the clock does not stop. Frequent breaks
  may be flagged as an exam security concern.

I accidentally exit the test or my internet/equipment fails.
  Restart or reconnect as quickly as possible. Use the same Zoom link to re-join.
  Call the ABO to let us know you're working to get back online. If the issue is
  with your audio or video, a troubleshooter can help. If your sound drops, use
  the chat to ask your examiner to call for help.

My examiner's equipment fails.
  The other examiner in your room or another member of the exam team will pick up
  where the previous examiner left off. A troubleshooter will also step in to assist.
